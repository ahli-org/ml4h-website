## The reviewer's role

ML4H reviews are the backbone of the community. As a reviewer, your job
is to help the program chairs make good decisions and to give authors
actionable feedback — regardless of the outcome.

### What to evaluate

- **Relevance.** Is the work within scope for ML4H?
- **Novelty.** What is new relative to prior work?
- **Technical soundness.** Are the methods correct and well-described?
- **Experimental rigor.** Are baselines appropriate? Are results reproducible?
- **Clinical validity.** Does the clinical context justify the approach?
- **Clarity.** Is the paper well-written and easy to follow?

### Tone and constructiveness

Reviews should be respectful and professional. Critique the work, not the
authors. Be specific: if something is unclear or incorrect, say exactly
what and why. Offer suggestions for improvement where possible.

### Conflicts of interest

Declare any conflicts of interest to the area chairs immediately.
Do not review work from authors at your institution, former advisees
or advisors, or anyone with whom you have a close personal relationship.
